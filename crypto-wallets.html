Mobile Wallets

Coinomi Wallet: Coinomi’s UI system is easy to use and doesn't require any extra tools or steps other than setting up an account with them. They also have a desktop version which is just as easy and intuitive to use. They support over 1,700 different cryptocurrencies/blockchains. Coinomi has been around since 2014 and not a single wallet has been hacked. Overall a great choice to have a safe and easy-to-use mobile wallet.

Exodus Wallet: These wallets are strictly through a smartphone and there are quite a bit to choose from. We have our own personal top 3 choices, but we strongly advise that you do your own research as well. Exodus wallet is another great choice that has a stunning UI/UX experience. Exodus offers both mobile and desktop versions as well. Exodus only offers 155+ cryptocurrencies but for most people that is more cryptocurrencies than they will ever own in their lifetime. They are partnered with Trezor to offer an integrated cold wallet and mobile wallet. Exodus was also voted best for beginners mobile wallet by Investopedia.

Trust Wallet: Last but not least is the Trust wallet. Having over 25 million users, trust wallet seems to have quite a lot of people that use and trust it. The one major selling point of the Trust wallet is that it supports over 53 different blockchains as well as over 1 million cryptocurrencies and assets. For the best needs of a first-time crypto investor, the Trust wallet might not be the best choice but for more experienced investors and people looking to buy very niche projects, this wallet is great for storing those assets securely.

The overall winner is the Exodus wallet for mobile use because of its great UI/UX design and great integration with other platforms and cold wallets.

Cold Wallets

Trezor 
Trezor Model T: This is Trezor’s premium cold wallet that runs around $215. Trezor provides a great UI experience and is extremely easy to use. Supports almost all cryptocurrencies.


Trezor One: This is Trezor's affordable option that runs about $70. It has a Monochrome display with 2 buttons. It's a simple/basic cold wallet but it gets the job done. This wallet supports over 1000 cryptocurrencies.
- We recommend visiting their website and reading all about their wallets. It is also recommended to buy directly from Trezor’s website and not from a 3rd party due to the fact that there are scammers that make fakes. CoinArk is not responsible for any purchases that result in the loss of funds.

Ledger
Ledger Nano X: The Nano X is the ledger's premium cold wallet that runs about $150. Ledger provides a good UI experience along with a mobile app that pairs with their software so you can use it on the go. They support over 5,500 cryptocurrencies and support over 100 cryptos on the Nano X at one time. It's one screen and 2 button design.

Ledger Nano S: The Nano S is their budget version that runs $60. There is not much difference in design or features other than it supports considerably fewer cryptos at one time. 5 to 6 cryptocurrencies can be stored at one time. If you have lots of different types of cryptocurrency then this might not be the best choice for you but if you only carry a few then this will offer plenty for a great price.

Ledger Nano S pro: Ledger is working on a new cold wallet. It is currently in the works and the release date is unknown. If you go to their website you can sign up and they will send you info about it.
- Same as our recommendation for Trezor, go check out Ledger's official website and strongly consider ordering from their official website to prevent any potential of being scammed. Again this is not financial advice and we are not responsible for any loss of funds from scams or misuse of these wallets.

If you are consistently buying bitcoin and other cryptocurrency, it is highly recommended to invest in a cold wallet and learn how to use them. They provide the best security for your crypto due to the fact that once you store your crypto on the cold wallet it's offline and no one can hack or steal it unless they physically steal the cold wallet and even then they would need your seed phrases and passwords. There are more than 2 choices, we feel as though these are the most popular and well reviewed choices.
